Executive Summary

In this technology infused age, the dewey decimal system, commonly found in brick and mortar libraries and bookshops, is becoming increasingly less used and known.

The technology age of smartphones, tablets, laptops, and desktops need better ways of finding and categorizing/sorting books.

LibML is the solution to our book sorting and categorizing problem. LibML provides the most effective and efficient way to both sort and find books online. All information is clearly shown, thereâ€™s no drawers to pull down, no dewey decimal system to know. LibML can be used to essentially create a log of every book ever written through author, title, ISBN, and more.

Element Structure

Books contain three major details:

  1. ISBN
  2. Detail
  3. Writers
These three, in order from most specific down to still specific but somewhat general play key factors in organizing books. As for more detail, ISBN cannot be broken down. Detail can be broken down into name+, volume?, edition, publishing date, and publishing house. Lastly, Writers can be broken into author+ and editor?.

Attributes

Some attributes that can added on to certain elements are as follows:

  • The edition element has the attribute which corresponds to what medium the books is printed on. The type may be Softcover, Hardcover, and eBook.
  • The isbn element has the attribute of type which may contain either 10 or 13.
These books are listed as their own entity and not per actual copy, since this is for reference, not sale, of books. Thus, the attribute of condition does not apply.
